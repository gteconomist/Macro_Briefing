# Daily Macro Briefing — Thursday, May 21, 2026

## Top of mind

The long end continues to cheapen at a pace that demands attention: the 10-year closed at 4.67% on 5/19 (up 6 bps from 4.61%), the 30-year at 5.18% (up 4 bps), and the 2-year at 4.13% (up 6 bps), leaving the 10s/3m spread at +92 bps as of 5/20. WTI at $112.25 and Brent at $116.73 — both up roughly $3 on the week through Monday's close — are adding a stagflationary undertone that the rate selloff is amplifying. The FOMC Minutes (released yesterday) are the interpretive lens for all of this; the key question is how explicitly the committee framed the tension between above-target inflation and softening activity, and whether any members signaled that the current 3.50–3.75% target range is still appropriate given where energy is.

## Yesterday's releases (Wed 5/20)

- **FOMC Minutes (May meeting):** Released 14:00 ET. No FRED scalar to report, but the Minutes are the week's highest-signal release — watch for characterization of the inflation risk balance, any dissent language around the pace of cuts, and how members assessed tariff and energy price pass-through into the inflation outlook.

## Today's calendar (Thu 5/21, ET)

- **Housing Starts, 08:30** — April starts came in at 1,465K (SAAR) vs. 1,507K in March; today's print will either confirm a nascent softening in residential construction or flag that March was the outlier. Permits were 1,442K in April vs. 1,363K in March, so the pipeline is less alarming than starts — watch whether starts converge up toward permits or permits follow starts lower.
- **Philadelphia Fed Manufacturing, 08:30** — The Empire State index for May surged to +19.6 from +11.0; Philly flipped sharply the other direction in April (+26.7 → −0.4), making this month's read critical for assessing whether mid-Atlantic manufacturing is genuinely rolling or whether April was noise. A sustained negative would matter alongside the oil price signal.
- **Initial Jobless Claims, 08:30** — The week ending 5/16 printed 209K, down from 212K — still low, but continuing claims (1,782K for the week of 5/9) crept up 6K. Claims have been unusually stable; any break above ~230K would shift the labor market narrative.

## This week ahead

- **Fri 5/22:** Consumer Sentiment (Final, U-Mich, 10:00) — The preliminary March read was 53.3 (down from 56.6 in February) with 1-year inflation expectations at 3.8%; the final revision will confirm whether that deterioration and the inflation expectations tick-up held through the full survey window. With energy prices elevated, any upward revision to the inflation expectations component is the number to watch.

## Fed watch

Target range is 3.50–3.75%, effective fed funds at 3.62% (5/19), SOFR at 3.50% (5/20) — both running near the floor of the corridor, consistent with ample reserves. Yesterday's Minutes are the week's primary Fed signal; the committee has been threading a needle between residual inflation pressure and emerging demand softness, and the Minutes will clarify how much disagreement exists about that balance at current rates. No FOMC meeting, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release appears in the remaining week-ahead calendar. With the 30-year Treasury at 5.18% and WTI north of $112, the market is doing some of the Fed's tightening work on the long end — but oil at these levels also raises the probability that core PCE (last print 129.28 for March) stays sticky well into Q3, which limits the Fed's room to cut even if the activity data soften further.
