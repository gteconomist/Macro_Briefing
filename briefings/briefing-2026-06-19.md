# Daily Macro Briefing — Friday, June 19, 2026

## Top of mind

The Iran nuclear/sanctions deal is the dominant macro variable this week. The U.S. lifting its naval blockade and allowing ships into Iranian ports (per Vance) is already showing up in oil: WTI dropped from $88.62 to $84.65 and Brent from $88.64 to $84.36 between June 12 and June 15 — a ~$4 move on the week. Gas prices falling below $4 nationally for the first time since March feeds directly into near-term headline CPI, though core is the stickier story. The rates market is repricing simultaneously: the 2-year jumped 15 bps to 4.20% on June 17 while the 10-year moved 6 bps to 4.49%, steepening the curve and suggesting markets are wrestling with whether easing energy prices gives the Fed room or whether tariff pass-through keeps core elevated.
