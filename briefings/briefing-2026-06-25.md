# Daily Macro Briefing — Thursday, June 25, 2026

## Top of mind

Today is the single heaviest data day of the week — GDP second estimate, PCE, durable goods, and claims all drop at 8:30. The most consequential number will be May PCE: the April print showed headline PCE at 130.902 and core at 129.63, implying year-over-year runs still above target, and the Fed needs to see whether May extended the modest deceleration or reversed it. Meanwhile, Brent has slid sharply — from $80.46 on June 19 to $76.49 on June 22, a nearly $4 drop in three sessions — which, if sustained, provides some disinflationary cover but also signals softening global demand expectations. HY spreads ticked up 6 bps to 271 on Tuesday, a modest but worth-watching move given how compressed they've been.

## Yesterday's releases (Wed 6/24)

- **New Home Sales (May):** FRED reports May new home sales at 580K SAAR, down from 626K in April — a 7.4% monthly decline that snaps back below the 600K threshold and reinforces the picture of a rate-sensitive housing sector grinding lower; starts already fell hard in May (1,177K vs. 1,392K in April), so the demand side is now confirming the supply-side pullback.

## Today's calendar (Thu 6/25, ET)

- **GDP (Q1 second estimate), 8:30** — Watch the revision to the advance print; any move toward or below zero on real GDP growth will sharpen recession-timing conversations heading into Q2.
- **Personal Income & Outlays (PCE), 8:30** — The primary inflation read for the Fed; May core PCE month-over-month and the year-over-year level are the numbers that matter most for the July FOMC setup.
- **Durable Goods Advance (May), 8:30** — Core capex (nondefense ex-aircraft orders) is the signal; a second consecutive soft print would reinforce the narrative that business fixed investment is stalling under policy uncertainty.
- **Initial Jobless Claims (week ending ~6/21), 8:30** — Prior week came in at 226K (down from 230K); continued claims at 1.810M are drifting higher and deserve as much attention as the headline. Watch for any upward break above 235K on initials.

## This week ahead

- **Fri 6/26:** Consumer Sentiment Final (U of Mich, 10:00) — Preliminary April read was a weak 49.8 with 1-year inflation expectations at 4.7%; the final June read will show whether sentiment has stabilized or deteriorated further, and the inflation expectations component remains a direct Fed input.

## Fed watch

The target range is 3.50–3.75%, with the effective fed funds rate at 3.63% and SOFR at 3.62% — both trading neatly at the midpoint, no technical pressure. The 2-year yield dropped 8 bps to 4.16% on Tuesday while the 10-year was essentially unchanged at 4.50%, compressing the 2s10s modestly; the 10Y–3M spread narrowed to 56 bps on Wednesday from 65 bps the prior day, still positive but moving in the wrong direction. No FOMC statement, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release appears in the calendar for the next five business days. Today's PCE print is the decisive pre-July-meeting data point — if May core comes in hotter than expected, the case for holding at the July meeting solidifies further; a soft print reopens the cut-or-hold debate at the margins.
