## Top of mind

The dominant macro story is the new wave of U.S. tariffs hitting dozens of countries simultaneously, landing against a backdrop of an active military conflict with Iran that is already commanding congressional attention. The tariff shock compounds an existing risk-off bid: equities sold off sharply Thursday — S&P 500 -1.21% to 7,408, Nasdaq -2.15% to 25,138, DJIA -0.97% to 51,712 — while the 10-year Treasury yield ticked up to 4.67% and the 30-year hit 5.15%, suggesting the market is pricing stagflation risk rather than a flight-to-safety rally. Gold at $4,127 (+0.87% overnight) reinforces that read.

## Fed watch

The target range sits at 3.50–3.75%, with the effective fed funds rate at 3.63% and SOFR at 3.62% — both hugging the midpoint cleanly. The curve has steepened: the 2-year is at 4.31%, the 10-year at 4.67%, the 30-year at 5.15%, and the 10Y–3M spread has turned positive at +76 bps — a notable shift if sustained. No FOMC statement, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release appears in the calendar for the next five business days. The tariff escalation and Iran conflict put the Fed in an increasingly uncomfortable position: credit spreads remain remarkably contained (IG OAS 78 bps, HY 268 bps), so financial conditions haven't tightened enough to prompt easing, but the stagflationary supply shock from new tariffs argues against any near-term cut.
