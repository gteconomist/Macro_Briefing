# Daily Macro Briefing — Thursday, June 11, 2026

## Top of mind

May CPI dropped yesterday and equity markets sold off hard anyway — S&P 500 fell 1.6% to 7,267, DJIA lost 953 points to 49,919, Nasdaq off 2.0% — suggesting investors read the print as insufficient cover for near-term Fed cuts rather than as relief. The geopolitical overlay is intensifying: the U.S. military fired on an Iranian oil tanker, which with WTI already at $95.00 and Brent at $97.46 keeps the energy-driven inflation tail risk squarely on the table. Today's PPI and claims data will determine whether yesterday's market reaction was an overreaction to a noisy CPI or the beginning of a repricing of the path to easing.

---

## Yesterday's releases (Wed 6/10)

- **CPI (May):** Headline index 333.979, up from 332.407 in April (+1.572 index points, roughly +0.47% m/m). Core (ex-food & energy) 336.121 vs. 335.423 (+0.21% m/m). The core read is relatively contained, but the headline acceleration — likely energy-driven given WTI near $95 — was enough to rattle markets. Year-over-year context depends on where the 2025 base sits, but the month-over-month pace on headline is running well above what gets the Fed comfortable.

---

## Today's calendar (Thu 6/11, ET)

- **PPI, 8:30** — Watch the final-demand services component and the pipeline pressures in intermediate goods; with WTI at $95, goods PPI is almost certainly elevated and the question is whether service-sector margins are passing costs forward or absorbing them.
- **Initial Jobless Claims, 8:30** — Last print was 225K (week of 5/30), up notably from 212K the prior week; a second consecutive elevated read would start to shift the labor narrative meaningfully. Continuing claims (1.777M for the week of 5/23) are already edging lower, so the divergence between initial and continuing bears watching.

---

## This week ahead

- **Fri 6/12:** Consumer Sentiment — Preliminary Michigan read for June; the April final was 49.8 with 1-year inflation expectations at 4.7%, both deeply concerning. A further deterioration in sentiment or another inflation expectations spike would compound the stagflationary read from this week's CPI/PPI sequence and limit Fed optionality further.

---

## Fed watch

Target range holds at 3.50–3.75%, effective fed funds at 3.62%, SOFR at 3.60% — both hugging the midpoint of the band with no drift. The 2s/10s spread is 40 bps (4.13/4.53), and the 10Y/3M spread has ticked up to +76 bps, signaling a curve that has steepened modestly but is not yet pricing aggressive easing near-term. The 30-year at 5.01% is notable — the long end is effectively at par with the psychological 5% threshold, reflecting fiscal premium and sticky inflation expectations rather than growth enthusiasm. No FOMC statement, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release appears in the week-ahead calendar, so the next policy signal comes from the data flow itself: a hot PPI this morning following yesterday's CPI acceleration would further compress the probability of a July cut.
