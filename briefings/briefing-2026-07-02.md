# Daily Macro Briefing — Thursday, July 02, 2026

## Top of mind

The June Employment Report drops at 8:30 this morning — the dominant data event of the week and, given the macro backdrop, probably the month. The labor market has been the last pillar holding the soft-landing narrative: May nonfarm payrolls came in at 159,001K (+172K m/m), unemployment steady at 4.3%, and average hourly earnings ticking up to $32.31. Against that, the 10Y has backed up to 4.44% (from 4.38% Monday), the 2Y to 4.14%, and the T10Y3M spread has widened to +63 bps — markets are pricing something in. The Iran-Saudi rift headlined by the NYT adds a geopolitical overlay that's already keeping WTI above $71.

## Yesterday's releases (Wed 7/1)

- **ISM Manufacturing (June):** No FRED print in payload for today's release — watch for the reported index level. Context: the NY Fed's business conditions index collapsed from 19.6 in May to 5.7 in June, while the Philly Fed's equivalent swung from -0.4 to +10.3, sending mixed regional signals into this national read. Manufacturing has been the soft underbelly of the expansion; any sub-48 headline number will reinforce that.

## Today's calendar (Thu 7/2, ET)

- **Employment Report, 8:30** — The headline number. With UNRATE stuck at 4.3% for two consecutive months and CIVPART flat at 61.8%, the market needs to see whether May's +172K payroll gain was a genuine floor or a one-month aberration. AHE at $32.31 (+$0.08 m/m) will matter for the wage-price read; a print above +0.3% m/m would complicate the Fed's path.
- **Initial Jobless Claims, 8:30** — Released simultaneously with NFP. The June 20 week printed 215K, a sharp drop from 227K the prior week; continuing claims edged up to 1.821M (week of 6/13). Watch whether this week's initial claims confirm the downward move or reverse it — persistent sub-220K readings undercut any "cooling labor market" argument.
- **Factory Orders (Full Report), 10:00** — Durable goods capex orders (ex-defense, ex-aircraft) are the tell for business investment intentions. With industrial production barely moving (+0.14 pts to 102.65) and capacity utilization at 76.2%, any softness in core orders would deepen the manufacturing-weakness story.

## This week ahead

- **Fri 7/3:** **ISM Services (10:00)** — With manufacturing signaling soft, the services sector is carrying the bulk of GDP growth. The June read will be closely watched; any move below 50 would be a material downside surprise given that services have been the ballast of the expansion. Markets are on a holiday schedule ahead of July 4th — expect thin liquidity to amplify any print.

## Fed watch

Target range is 3.50–3.75% (DFEDTARU 3.75, DFEDTARL 3.50), with the effective fed funds rate at 3.63% and SOFR at 3.68% — both sitting comfortably mid-range, no technical pressure. The CFNAI slipped to -0.10 in May from +0.19 in April, the GDPNow tracker has Q1 at 1.19% and fading, and Michigan sentiment is at 44.8 with 1-year inflation expectations nudging to 4.8% — a stagflationary mix that gives the Committee no clean off-ramp. No FOMC meeting, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release appears in the week-ahead calendar, so the Employment Report this morning is the primary input for the July 29 decision. A strong NFP (+200K+, AHE firm) essentially takes July off the table; a soft print below +125K reopens the cuts debate heading into the summer.
