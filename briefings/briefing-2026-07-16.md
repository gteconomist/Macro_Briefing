## Top of mind

The inflation picture is the dominant story heading into Thursday's data cluster. June CPI came in at 332.568 — down from May's 333.979, a meaningful month-over-month decline — while core CPI was essentially flat (336.065 vs. 336.121). PPI for June also printed below May (156.566 vs. 157.001), reinforcing a disinflationary read across both headline measures. That sets the table for today's Retail Sales print: if consumer spending held up in June alongside softening prices, the soft-landing narrative gets another data point; if it didn't, the demand-side deceleration starts looking more structural.

## Fed watch

The target range remains 3.50–3.75%, with the effective fed funds rate at 3.63% and SOFR at 3.64% — both trading cleanly within the corridor. The 10Y/3M spread has turned decisively positive at +72 bps (vs. +74 bps the prior day), a meaningful shift from the prolonged inversion that characterized 2023–2024. Nothing on the `calendar_week_ahead` flags an FOMC meeting, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release in the next five business days. The 2s/10s spread implied by the FRED data (~40 bps) and the compressed IG spread (OAS at 79 bps, flat on the day) suggest financial conditions remain accommodative despite the policy rate sitting well above neutral — the key variable to watch is whether today's Retail Sales and Claims prints give the Committee cover to stay on hold through September or force a recalibration of the cutting path.
