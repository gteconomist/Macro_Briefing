# Daily Macro Briefing — Monday, July 06, 2026

## Top of mind

The June employment report — released Friday on the holiday-shortened week — is the dominant macro signal entering this week. Payrolls came in at 158,984K, a +57K month-over-month gain, with unemployment ticking down to 4.2% from 4.3% and average hourly earnings rising to $32.38 (+$0.07 MoM). The participation rate slipped 30 bps to 61.5%, which partially explains the unemployment decline. The headline is soft but not alarming: the labor market is cooling at a measured pace, consistent with the Fed's on-hold posture. The rate reaction was modest — the 10-year moved to 4.48% (+4 bps from 6/30) and the 2-year to 4.17% (+3 bps), leaving the 10Y–3M spread at +67 bps, steepening slightly but still well below levels historically associated with robust expansion.

## Fed watch

The target range remains 3.50–3.75%, with the effective fed funds rate at 3.63% and SOFR at 3.66% — both trading cleanly inside the corridor. The FOMC Minutes on Wednesday (7/8) are the key near-term read; the June meeting occurred against a backdrop of still-elevated services inflation (core PCE index up from 129.667 to 130.082 MoM in May), a softening but not broken labor market, and persistent tariff-driven price uncertainty, so the minutes should reveal how the committee is weighting those crosscurrents against the case for a resumption of cuts. No SEP refresh, Beige Book, or additional FOMC-related releases appear in this week's calendar.
