# Daily Macro Briefing — Tuesday, June 09, 2026

## Top of mind

The Israel-Iran escalation is the dominant macro variable right now. WTI has already moved from $91.16 to $95.96 (as of June 1 FRED data) and Brent from $92.88 to $98.29 — both within striking distance of $100 — and that was before the latest round of strikes. With U.S. carriers reporting $6.5B in April fuel costs and IATA slashing its global profit forecast nearly in half, the energy-price-to-inflation transmission channel is live. The 10-year closed at 4.55% on June 5 (up 8 bps from June 4), and the 30-year has crossed 5.01%, reflecting both the inflation risk premium and ongoing fiscal concerns — the curve is now unambiguously bear-steepening.

## Fed watch

The target range holds at 3.50–3.75%, with the effective fed funds rate at 3.62% and SOFR at 3.63% — both essentially anchored at the middle of the band. The 10Y3M spread has turned positive at +76 bps (June 8), a notable shift from the prolonged inversion, which historically complicates the recession signal but also reflects the market pricing in a higher-for-longer posture. No FOMC meeting, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release appears in the calendar through the end of this week. Wednesday's CPI print is the single most consequential input to the June FOMC calculus: a hot number — especially if headline is pulled up by energy — will reinforce the case for an extended pause, while a soft core read would revive the debate about whether the current 3.62% effective rate is sufficiently restrictive given the geopolitical inflation risk now building in the energy complex.
