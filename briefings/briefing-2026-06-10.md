# Daily Macro Briefing — Wednesday, June 10, 2026

## Top of mind

May CPI drops at 8:30 this morning and it's the dominant data event of the week. The inflation backdrop coming in is not benign: WTI has jumped to $95.96 from $91.16 at the end of May, Brent is at $98.29, and Michigan 1-year inflation expectations in April were already running at 4.7% — up sharply from 3.8% in March. April headline CPI was +0.64% MoM on the index (332.407 vs. 330.293), and core was +0.38% — not negligible. A second consecutive hot print today would meaningfully narrow the Fed's runway for any near-term easing and push the long end higher; the 30-year is already at 5.03%.

## Fed watch

Target range is 3.50–3.75%, with the effective fed funds rate at 3.62% and SOFR at 3.63% — both pinned cleanly inside the corridor. The 10Y3M spread has turned positive at +74 bps (up from +76 bps Monday, essentially flat), so the inversion that persisted through much of the hiking cycle is gone, though the curve steepening story is now being driven more by term premium at the long end than by near-term easing expectations. Nothing in the `calendar_week_ahead` payload flags an FOMC decision, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release in the next five business days. Today's CPI and Thursday's PPI will be the primary inputs shaping the policy debate; with oil above $95 and inflation expectations already elevated, the burden of proof for any cut has risen materially since Q1.
