# Daily Macro Briefing — Thursday, June 04, 2026

## Top of mind

Oil is the most acute cross-cutting pressure this morning. WTI jumped to $95.96 and Brent to $98.29 as of June 1 — a ~$4.80 and ~$5.40 move, respectively, off the prior Friday close — putting both benchmarks within striking distance of $100 and reopening the question of whether energy re-accelerates goods and transportation inflation just as the Fed is assessing cut cadence. That comes against a backdrop where April core PCE is already running warm (the index at 129.63, up from 129.32 in March) and University of Michigan 1-year inflation expectations ticked to 4.7% from 3.8% in March. Equities sold off hard yesterday — S&P 500 down 56 points to 7,553.68, DJIA off 621 to 50,687 — suggesting markets are repricing that confluence.

## Today's calendar (Thu 6/4, ET)

- **Productivity and Costs, 8:30** — Q1 unit labor costs are the key line; an upside print tightens the Fed's cost-push calculus at a moment when energy is adding to the inflation stack. Watch nonfarm business productivity for signal on whether the tight-labor-market efficiency gains of recent years are holding.
- **Initial Jobless Claims, 8:30** — Prior week (5/23) printed 215K, up from 210K the week before; continuing claims (5/16) at 1.786M and drifting higher. A third consecutive rise in initials would sharpen the picture of gradual labor market softening heading into tomorrow's NFP.

## This week ahead

- **Fri 6/5:** **Employment Report** (BLS, 8:30) — the week's marquee release; April payrolls were +115K (PAYEMS 158,736K vs. 158,621K), unemployment held at 4.3%, average hourly earnings at $32.23. May consensus will anchor around whether that modest pace continues or whether the uptick in claims and softer JOLTS trajectory (April openings rebounded to 7,618K from a weak 6,887K in March, but the trend is choppy) portend a cleaner deceleration. **Consumer Credit** (Fed, 15:00) — revolving vs. nonrevolving split will inform whether the April retail sales bounce (RSAFS +$3.7B to $757.1B) was debt-financed.

## Fed watch

Target range is 3.50–3.75%, effective fed funds at 3.62% as of June 2, SOFR at 3.63% — both printing cleanly inside the corridor. The 10Y–3M spread has widened to +71 bps (from +69 bps the prior day), so the curve continues its bear-steepening normalization but remains well off deeply inverted territory. No FOMC statement, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release appears in the week-ahead calendar. The Friday Employment Report is the decisive near-term input: a sub-100K print with stable unemployment would likely cement a July cut discussion, while a number north of 175K alongside tomorrow's productivity/ULC data — if ULC comes in hot — probably pushes the first cut debate back toward September and keeps the Fed on hold while it watches whether $98 Brent passes through into June CPI.
