# Daily Macro Briefing — Friday, July 10, 2026

## Top of mind

June's jobs report — released last Friday — remains the dominant macro signal: nonfarm payrolls added only 57K (158,984K vs. 158,927K prior), unemployment ticked down to 4.2% from 4.3%, and average hourly earnings edged up to $32.38. That combination — near-stall in hiring alongside a participation rate that slipped 30 bps to 61.5% — complicates the Fed's calculus heading into the second half of the year. Overnight, no data materially changed the picture, though the AI-driven M&A surge (NYT reports $3.2T in deal volume) is keeping equity risk appetite elevated: S&P 500 closed Thursday at 7,543.64, up 0.8% on the session, with Nasdaq at 26,206.89, +1.3%.
