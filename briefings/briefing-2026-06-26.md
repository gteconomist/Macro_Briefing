# Daily Macro Briefing — Friday, June 26, 2026

## Top of mind

Thursday delivered an unusually dense data bundle — GDP, PCE, durable goods, and claims all at 8:30 — and the bond market responded: the 10-year fell 9 bps to 4.41% and the 2-year dropped 5 bps to 4.11%, with the 30-year shedding 8 bps to 4.86%. The PCE print is the most consequential piece: headline PCE moved from 130.938 to 131.527 (+0.45% m/m) and core PCE from 129.667 to 130.082 (+0.32% m/m) — still running above the Fed's 2% target on an annualized basis but not accelerating. Apple's announced price increases on Macs and iPads, attributed to memory chip shortages, are a live reminder that tariff- and supply-chain-driven goods re-inflation is not purely a theoretical risk.
