# Daily Macro Briefing — Tuesday, July 07, 2026

## Top of mind

The June labor market report (released last Thursday, pre-holiday) showed nonfarm payrolls at 158,984K — a gain of just 57K over May — with unemployment ticking down to 4.2% but participation slipping 30 bps to 61.5%. That deceleration in job creation is the dominant macro narrative heading into this week. Overnight headlines carry no macro-relevant developments; the focus shifts squarely to today's JOLTS print and Wednesday's FOMC Minutes for signals on whether the Fed sees the labor market softening as cyclical drift or something more concerning.

- **JOLTS (May), 10:00** — Job openings came in at 7,594K for May (latest available), up marginally from 7,585K in April, keeping the V/U ratio barely above 1. ## This week ahead

- **Wed 7/8:** FOMC Minutes (14:00), Consumer Credit (15:00) — Minutes are the marquee item; the most recent consumer credit data through April show total credit at $5,153B (+$20.7B MoM), driven almost equally by revolving (+$11.6B) and non-revolving (+$9.1B). May data drop Wednesday.

Housing remains under pressure — starts collapsed to 1,177K in May from 1,392K in April.
- **Fri 7/10:** Consumer Sentiment Preliminary (10:00, UMich) — Michigan sentiment was 44.8 in May (latest FRED read); one-year inflation expectations edged up to 4.8%. A further deterioration would tighten the stagflation framing heading into summer.

## Fed watch

Target range is 3.50–3.75% (DFEDTARU/DFEDTARL as of 7/6); the effective fed funds rate is 3.63% (as of 7/3) and SOFR is 3.64% (7/2) — both sitting comfortably mid-range. The 10Y/3M spread has narrowed slightly to +61 bps (from +67 bps on 7/2), still positive but well off levels that would signal clear accommodation runway. Wednesday's FOMC Minutes are the key near-term event — with core PCE running above target (May index: 130.08 vs. 129.67 in April) and the labor market now showing a meaningful payroll deceleration, the Minutes will be read closely for any shift in the balance-of-risks language or evolving views on the timing of the next cut.
