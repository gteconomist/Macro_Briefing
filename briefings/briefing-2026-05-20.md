# Daily Macro Briefing — Wednesday, May 20, 2026

## Top of mind

The long end continues to drift higher — the 10-year at 4.61% and the 30-year at 5.14% (both up 2 bps from Friday) — while the 10yr/3mo spread has widened to +100 bps from +93 bps the prior day, signaling a steeper curve driven by term premium rather than growth expectations. WTI at $101.56 and Brent at $106.11 (as of May 11) keep the inflation re-acceleration risk alive, and that backdrop makes today's FOMC minutes the focal point: markets will be parsing the May meeting discussion for how seriously the committee weighed the oil-driven upside inflation risk against any softening in labor and sentiment data.

## Fed watch

Target range is 3.50–3.75%, effective fed funds at 3.63%, SOFR at 3.51% — both well-anchored within the band. The curve steepening (2s at 4.07%, 10s at 4.61%, 30s at 5.14%) is doing incremental tightening work at the long end without any policy action. Today's FOMC minutes are the only Fed event on the calendar through week's end; no Beige Book, SEP, or additional FOMC decisions appear in the remaining week-ahead calendar. The core question embedded in the minutes will be whether the committee's reaction function has shifted enough to make a 2026 cut contingent on unambiguously softer inflation, or whether the labor softening signals — LFPR down a tenth, claims rising, JOLTs openings at 6,866K and still declining — are beginning to tip the balance.
