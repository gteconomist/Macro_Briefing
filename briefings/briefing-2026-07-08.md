# Daily Macro Briefing — Wednesday, July 08, 2026

## Top of mind

The Iran oil sanctions waiver revocation is the macro story to watch this morning. The U.S. pulled the waiver following tanker attacks, which puts upward pressure on crude from already-elevated levels — Brent and WTI were last reported at $71.59 and $71.87 respectively (as of June 29), and any meaningful supply disruption from Iranian flows would complicate the Fed's inflation path at an already delicate moment. That feeds directly into today's FOMC Minutes (14:00 ET), where the committee's read on tariff and energy pass-through will be scrutinized for any shift in the cutting timeline. Equities softened yesterday — S&P 500 down to 7,503.85 from 7,537.43, Nasdaq off roughly 302 points — suggesting markets are repricing geopolitical risk at the margin.

## Today's calendar (Wed 7/8, ET)

- **FOMC Minutes, 14:00** — The June meeting left the target range at 3.50–3.75%. Watch for the degree of internal disagreement on the pace of further cuts, how explicitly tariff-driven inflation is being treated as transitory vs. persistent, and any discussion of balance sheet trajectory. Given the Iran headline, the committee's energy price sensitivity is immediately relevant.
- **Consumer Credit (G.19), 15:00** — FRED has April data already: total consumer credit $5,153.1B, up ~$20.7B from March ($5,132.4B). Revolving credit led the move, +$11.6B to $1,348.7B; non-revolving +$9.1B to $3,804.4B. Today's May release will be the first look at whether that acceleration in revolving (i.e., credit card) balances extended into the spring — important context for the consumption picture given softening sentiment (U Michigan at 44.8 in May).

## Fed watch

The target range sits at 3.50–3.75%, with the effective fed funds rate at 3.63% and SOFR at 3.63% — both hugging the midpoint cleanly. The 10Y–3M spread has widened slightly to +69 bps (from +61 bps the prior day), reflecting modest steepening as the front end holds and the long end drifts — 10Y at 4.48%, 30Y at 4.99%. The 2s10s is +35 bps (4.13%/4.48%), a curve that has normalized but is not signaling aggressive re-acceleration. Today's FOMC Minutes are the week's key Fed event; with the Iran sanctions development now layered on top of still-elevated PPI (+1.648 points, April to May) and PCE core grinding higher ($130.082 vs. $129.667), the minutes will be parsed closely for how much tolerance the committee has for holding if energy feeds back into headline inflation.
