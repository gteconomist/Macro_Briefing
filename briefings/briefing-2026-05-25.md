# Daily Macro Briefing — Monday, May 25, 2026

## Top of mind

The dominant macro story remains the energy price surge and its inflation implications. WTI at $112.25 and Brent at $116.73 — up roughly $3 week-over-week as of 5/18 — are well above levels consistent with the Fed's path back to 2% PCE, with the most recent core PCE print (March) already running at an annualized pace that keeps the Fed sidelined. Iran peace talk headlines this morning are moving oil lower in Asian trading, which bears watching: a durable geopolitical de-escalation would significantly change the near-term inflation arithmetic, but Trump's own framing — "we won't rush into a deal" — signals no near-term resolution. ## Fed watch

The 2s/10s spread has widened slightly — 2-year at 4.08% (up 4 bps from 5/20) against the 10-year unchanged at 4.57% — while the 30-year at 5.10% continues to signal term premium pressure that the Fed cannot easily dismiss. No FOMC meeting, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release appears in the week-ahead calendar, so the Fed is in a data-watching posture; Wednesday's PCE print will be the primary input into how aggressively the easing-vs-holding debate shifts internally heading into June.
