## Top of mind

## Fed watch

The target range holds at 3.50–3.75%, with the effective fed funds rate at 3.62% and SOFR at 3.51%. The 2s10s spread sits at +49 bps (2-year at 4.08%, 10-year at 4.57%), and the 10Y-3M spread has turned positive at +88 bps — a meaningful shift from the prolonged inversion. Nothing in the calendar through next Friday flags an FOMC decision, minutes release, Beige Book, or SEP update, so the policy signal this week comes primarily from Thursday's PCE print. With Michigan inflation expectations at 4.7% and oil prices now carrying a geopolitical premium, the bar for any near-term easing has moved materially higher — the Fed needs sequential evidence that inflation is re-converging to target before it can credibly restart the cutting cycle.
