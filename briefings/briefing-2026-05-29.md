# Daily Macro Briefing — Friday, May 29, 2026

## Top of mind

Yesterday delivered a rare triple of high-tier releases on the same morning — GDP second estimate, PCE, and durable goods — and the inflation read is the one demanding attention. The April PCE price index came in at 130.902 (March: 130.381), and core PCE at 129.630 (March: 129.321), implying roughly +0.4% MoM on both headline and core — uncomfortably above the pace consistent with a return to 2%. That lands against a backdrop where the 30-year Treasury is still sitting at 5.01% and WTI has dropped from $100.35 to $97.63 in the span of a week, partly on drone strikes hitting shadow-fleet tankers in the Black Sea — a supply disruption story worth watching if it escalates.

## Fed watch

Target range holds at 3.50–3.75%; effective fed funds at 3.62%; SOFR at 3.63%, both unchanged. The April core PCE print (~+0.4% MoM on headline, ~+0.24% on core) does nothing to open the door for near-term cuts — the 30-year at 5.01% and the 10-year at 4.48% reflect a market that is pricing fiscal risk and persistent inflation simultaneously. The 10Y–3M spread at +76 bps (down from +80 bps a day prior) is no longer inverted, removing the mechanical recession signal, but that normalization is driven by term premium expansion rather than easing expectations. Neither the `calendar_week_ahead` nor the payload contains an FOMC meeting, minutes release, Beige Book, or SEP in the next five business days — next policy catalyst is data-dependent.
