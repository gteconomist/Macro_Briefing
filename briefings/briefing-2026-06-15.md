# Daily Macro Briefing — Monday, June 15, 2026

## Top of mind

The dominant macro development entering this week is a reported deal to end the Iran war, which is driving a sharp risk-on rotation — equities up, oil down — that is visible in Friday's close (S&P 500 at 7,431, +37 points on the day) and will likely set the tone for Monday's open. The geopolitical relief comes into a week that is almost entirely about the Fed: the FOMC statement drops Wednesday, and markets will be parsing it against a backdrop of still-elevated WTI ($95.00) and Brent ($97.46), a 10Y that rallied 10 bps on the week to 4.45%, and a 2s10s spread around +40 bps. Core CPI for May printed +0.21% m/m (334.0 vs. 332.4 prior index level), and University of Michigan 1-year inflation expectations jumped to 4.7% from 3.8% in March — the Fed has cover to hold and reason to stay cautious on the easing path.

## Fed watch

Target range is 3.50–3.75%, effective fed funds at 3.62%, SOFR at 3.60% — all stable and well within the corridor. The FOMC statement Wednesday is the pivotal event: the committee is navigating a genuine stagflation-adjacent tension — headline CPI index up ~1.57 points m/m in May, Michigan 1-year inflation expectations at 4.7% (a significant step up from 3.8% in March), against a labor market that is softening at the margin (claims drifting, UNRATE steady at 4.3%, participation flat at 61.8%). The Iran deal headline and associated oil pullback could reduce near-term energy-driven inflation pressure, but WTI at $95 and Brent at $97.46 remain elevated in absolute terms. The rate statement will need to thread the needle between acknowledging disinflation progress and keeping optionality to hold — any hint at a September cut is the market mover to watch.
