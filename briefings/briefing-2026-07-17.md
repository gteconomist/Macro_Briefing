## Top of mind

The dominant macro story is the U.S. military campaign against Iran and its escalation into Gulf state territory — oil's response has been sharp and fast. WTI jumped from $72.45 to $79.20 and Brent from $74.34 to $81.62 between July 10 and July 13 alone, a move of roughly $7–$9/bbl in three sessions. That's the primary upside risk to the disinflation story right now, especially given that headline CPI had already printed negative month-over-month in June (332.568 vs. 333.979 in May) and PPI also declined. A sustained oil shock would complicate the Fed's path even as underlying price data softens.
