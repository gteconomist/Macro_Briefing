# Daily Macro Briefing — Wednesday, June 03, 2026

## Top of mind

The labor market is sending mixed signals heading into Friday's employment report. Yesterday's JOLTS print showed job openings jumping to 7,618K in April from 6,887K in March — a 731K surge that pushes the openings-to-unemployed ratio back up and complicates any straightforward "softening labor market" narrative. At the same time, continuing claims hit 1,786K (week of 5/16), up 15K from the prior week, suggesting workers who lose jobs are taking longer to find new ones. The 2s/10s spread sits at 42 bps (4.05% / 4.47%) with both legs drifting higher, and WTI has slipped to $97.63 off the $100.35 print from the prior week — worth watching as a potential disinflationary tailwind heading into the next PCE cycle.

## Fed watch

The target range remains 3.25–3.50% (DFEDTARU 3.50, DFEDTARL 3.25), with the effective fed funds rate at 3.62% — sitting just above the ceiling, consistent with normal corridor mechanics — and SOFR at 3.65%, up 2 bps from the prior observation. The curve drift (2Y +7 bps over the past few trading days) suggests the market is nudging up its terminal rate expectations, or at minimum pulling back on near-term cut pricing. No FOMC meeting, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release appears in the calendar payload for the remainder of this week. Friday's employment report will be the primary input for the next policy assessment; a second consecutive month of 4.3% unemployment with a soft payroll print would likely reinforce a hold posture, while a rebound in hiring combined with the JOLTS opening surge could push the next cut further out on the strip.
