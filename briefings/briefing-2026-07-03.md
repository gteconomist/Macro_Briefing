# Daily Macro Briefing — Friday, July 03, 2026

## Top of mind

The June Employment Report — dropped yesterday morning — is the dominant story heading into a holiday-shortened Friday. PAYEMS shows nonfarm payrolls at 158,984K in June, up 57K from May's 158,927K, while unemployment ticked down to 4.2% from 4.3% and average hourly earnings rose to $32.38 from $32.31 (+$0.07 MoM). Participation slipped 30 bps to 61.5%, which is doing some of the work on the unemployment rate. The read is soft-but-not-alarming: enough hiring to keep the Fed on hold comfortably, not enough to close off rate-cut optionality later this year. The Treasury market pushed back modestly — the 10Y moved to 4.48% from 4.44% and the 30Y to 4.97% from 4.91% — suggesting the bond market read the jobs number as hotter than feared, or is still digesting fiscal supply dynamics.

## Yesterday's releases (Thu 7/2)

- **Employment Report (June):** Payrolls +57K to 158,984K; unemployment 4.2% (from 4.3%); AHE $32.38 (+$0.07); labor force participation 61.5% (−30 bps). Hiring is running below trend pace — enough to sustain expansion but not enough to tighten slack further, with the participation drop doing some flattering work on the headline unemployment rate.

## Today's calendar (Fri 7/3, ET)

- **ISM Services, 10:00** — After the NY Fed's business activity index collapsed from 19.6 to 5.7 in June, the services ISM is the key cross-check on whether the goods/manufacturing softness is spreading into the dominant sector of the economy. Watch the business activity, new orders, and prices-paid subcomponents; a sub-50 print or a sharp drop in new orders would materially shift the near-term growth narrative heading into the holiday weekend.

## This week ahead

- **Tue 7/7:** JOLTS (May) — The May opening count is already in hand from FRED at 7,594K (up marginally from 7,585K in April), but the BLS release will provide the full quits rate and layoffs breakdown. The quits rate is the cleanest real-time read on worker confidence and wage-bargaining power; any further drift down from already-subdued levels would reinforce the picture of a labor market losing dynamism.

## Fed watch

The target range holds at 3.50–3.75%, with the effective fed funds rate at 3.63% and SOFR at 3.66% — both sitting tidily inside the corridor with no operational stress. The 10Y3M spread is +67 bps (up from +63 bps the prior day), confirming the curve has been positively sloped for some time now, which removes one of the more politically salient recession signals. Nothing on the calendar for the next five business days touches FOMC communications — no minutes, no Beige Book, no SEP — so the June employment print is the last major input the market will chew on until next week's JOLTS and whatever Fedspeak resumes post-holiday. With payroll growth running soft, participation retreating, and the CFNAI at −0.1 in May, the balance of risk is drifting toward accommodation, but the 30Y at 4.97% signals that fiscal and term-premium concerns are keeping the long end from rallying into any nascent easing narrative.
