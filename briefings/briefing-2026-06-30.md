# Daily Macro Briefing — Tuesday, June 30, 2026

## Top of mind

The macro tape heading into Q3 is surprisingly resilient: S&P 500 closed Monday at 7,440 (+1.2% from Friday), the 10Y sits at 4.38%, and the 10Y–3M spread has widened back to +51 bps — curve steepening that looks more like a soft-landing repricing than a recession signal. The more pressing question is whether Friday's Employment Report (July 2) confirms that May's modest +172K payroll gain and a flat 4.3% unemployment rate represent a genuine plateau or the leading edge of softening. Today's ADP and JOLTS will set the table; April JOLTS already showed a notable bounce to 7,618K openings from 6,887K, so any retracement will attract attention.

## Fed watch

The target range remains 3.50–3.75%, with the effective fed funds rate at 3.63% and SOFR at 3.62% — both well within the corridor. The 2Y at 4.07% still sits roughly 32 bps above the upper bound, pricing in no imminent cut and a modest probability of further tightening or a prolonged hold. Nothing in the week's calendar (see above) includes an FOMC statement, minutes, Beige Book, or SEP release, so the policy signal this week comes entirely through the data — specifically Friday's Employment Report. Given that May's PCEPI and core PCE both ticked up (131.527 and 130.082 respectively, with core PCE up ~0.32% m/m), and UMich inflation expectations edged to 4.8% from 4.7%, the Fed has no obvious justification to accelerate cuts even if the labor data softens.
